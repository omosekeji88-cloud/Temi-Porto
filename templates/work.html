AS-OCT 3D Reconstruction and Atlas Modelling

Biomedical Engineering | Python | Registration | PCA | Atlas Modelling

Developed a 3D reconstruction and atlas-modelling pipeline for anterior-segment OCT scans. The project converted expert 2D annotations into 3D cornea, iris and lesion models, aligned patient-specific meshes using ICP/CPD registration, and applied PCA to analyse anatomical variation and map keratitis lesion distribution in a shared atlas space.

Selected Results

This is a selected snapshot of my final-year dissertation project, where I built a Python-based workflow for 3D reconstruction and statistical analysis of anterior-segment OCT data. The showcased outputs include the reconstruction pipeline, 3D cornea/iris/lesion models, ICP alignment visualisations, CPD metric tables, PCA scree plots and an atlas-space lesion-frequency map. Together, these demonstrate how I used Python-driven data processing, geometric registration and statistical modelling to transform 2D clinical annotations into interpretable 3D anatomical and pathology outputs.
